# Stakeholder Engagement Strategies by Quadrant

Use this reference in Step 4 when building the engagement plan. Match each stakeholder's Power/Interest Grid quadrant to the appropriate strategy below.

---

## Manage Closely (High Power, High Interest)

These stakeholders can make or break the project. They care deeply and have authority to act.

| Aspect | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| **Communication frequency** | Weekly or more; real-time for critical decisions |
| **Preferred channels** | Face-to-face meetings, 1:1 briefings, dedicated Slack/Teams channel |
| **Typical concerns** | Timeline, budget, strategic alignment, risk, resource allocation |
| **Engagement actions** | Involve in key decisions early; seek their input before presenting to wider group; schedule regular 1:1 check-ins; no surprises - escalate issues immediately |
| **Risk if neglected** | Project blocked, funding withdrawn, scope dictated without BA input |

**Examples**: Project sponsor, key business owner, executive champion

---

## Keep Satisfied (High Power, Low Interest)

These stakeholders have authority but aren't tracking details. They expect competence and minimal disruption.

| Aspect | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| **Communication frequency** | Biweekly to monthly; milestone-based updates |
| **Preferred channels** | Executive summary emails, dashboard reports, brief steering committee updates |
| **Typical concerns** | Cost overruns, timeline delays, reputational risk, compliance |
| **Engagement actions** | Send concise status updates highlighting risks and decisions needed; escalate only when their authority is required; respect their time - keep communications brief |
| **Risk if neglected** | Sudden intervention when they notice a problem; project deprioritized |

**Examples**: C-suite not directly sponsoring, department heads with budget authority, compliance officers

---

## Keep Informed (Low Power, High Interest)

These stakeholders care about the outcome but lack decision authority. Their engagement drives adoption.

| Aspect | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| **Communication frequency** | Weekly updates; open Q&A sessions |
| **Preferred channels** | Team meetings, newsletters, demo sessions, shared documentation |
| **Typical concerns** | How their work will change, training plans, timeline for rollout, input being heard |
| **Engagement actions** | Include in workshops and feedback sessions; demonstrate progress regularly; acknowledge their input explicitly; provide training timeline early |
| **Risk if neglected** | Low adoption, resistance to change, workarounds that undermine the solution |

**Examples**: End users, frontline staff, team leads, help desk, training team

---

## Monitor (Low Power, Low Interest)

These stakeholders are minimally affected. Keep them aware but don't over-invest.

| Aspect | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| **Communication frequency** | Monthly or milestone-based; ad-hoc as needed |
| **Preferred channels** | Project newsletter, shared workspace, intranet updates |
| **Typical concerns** | General awareness, potential future impact on their area |
| **Engagement actions** | Include in broadcast communications; monitor for changes in their interest level; be available if they reach out |
| **Risk if neglected** | Minimal in most cases; risk increases if their power or interest level changes |

**Examples**: Adjacent teams not directly affected, external observers, future-phase stakeholders

---

## Detecting Quadrant Shifts

Stakeholders move between quadrants. Watch for signals:

| Signal | Likely Shift |
|--------|-------------|
| Stakeholder starts asking detailed questions | Interest increasing - may move up |
| Stakeholder gets promoted or takes on new responsibility | Power increasing - may move left |
| Organizational restructuring | Both power and interest may change |
| Project enters their team's domain | Interest increasing |
| Budget review or audit announced | Power stakeholders become more interested |

Review and update the map at each project milestone or phase transition.
