# LinkedIn Post Generator

## Overview

This skill generates LinkedIn posts that stop the scroll, earn real engagement, and build authority in your professional niche.

**Critical distinction:** LinkedIn rewards professional vulnerability and tactical value. The voice is direct, personal, specific - but the format is tighter and every sentence must earn its place. The first two lines are everything.

## How It Works

### Step 1: Identify Hook Type

The first 1-2 lines determine whether anyone reads past "see more." Choose one hook:

**1. Number/Stat Hook** - Lead with a surprising or specific number
`"259 PRs. One developer. One month."`

**2. Personal Confession** - Admit something relatable
`"I spent two years optimizing my calendar. My output went up. My thinking got worse."`

**3. Contrarian Claim** - Contradict a common belief directly
`"Hustle culture didn't burn me out. Optimization did."`

**4. Bold Declarative** - Make a strong claim without softening it
`"Most tools fail in production because the prompt is doing too much work."`

**5. Scene-Setting** - Drop the reader into a specific moment
`"Last Tuesday I closed my laptop at 2pm and my work was done."`

Psychology: LinkedIn truncates posts at ~210 characters before "see more." The hook must create enough tension or curiosity to earn the click. Number hooks and confessions consistently outperform questions and generic advice hooks.

### Step 2: Choose Post Structure

**Structure A: Story > Lesson > Action**
Best for: personal experience, build-in-public, career moments
```
[Hook - personal scene or number]

[2-3 lines of story context - what happened]

[The turn - what you realized or learned]

[1-2 lines of lesson - the transferable insight]

[Closing - action or question]
```

**Structure B: Numbered Tactical List**
Best for: sharing frameworks, processes, tool breakdowns
```
[Hook - what the list delivers]

1. [Specific point]
2. [Specific point]
3. [Specific point]
(5 max - any more and you lose readers)

[One-line synthesis]

[CTA or question]
```

**Structure C: Contrarian Reframe**
Best for: challenging conventional wisdom, positioning
```
[Bold contrarian claim]

[Why the common belief exists]

[Why it's wrong - with evidence from your experience]

[What to do instead - specific]

[Closing]
```

**Structure D: Observation > Insight**
Best for: pattern recognition, authority-building
```
[What I've noticed...]

[2-3 examples or data points]

[The pattern - the insight others miss]

[Why it matters + what to do]
```

### Step 3: Write the Post

**Voice rules for LinkedIn:**

- Every sentence earns its place - tight, direct prose
- Open with "I", not "you" or "we"
- Single-line paragraphs for punches, 2-3 lines max for development
- White space is mandatory - no walls of text
- Specific numbers, timelines, outcomes - never vague
- Never: "Excited to share", "I'm humbled", "This journey", "We", corporate speak

**Length:** 150-300 words. Shorter than you think. Longer only if every sentence pulls weight.

**Links:** Never in the post body. Put the link in the first comment. Mention it at the end: "Link in first comment."

**Hashtags:** 3-5 at the end. Relevant to [your niche]. Example: #ProductManagement #BuildInPublic #StartupLife

### Step 4: Closing

Choose one:

**Engagement question** - genuine, not generic
`"What's the first workflow you automated? I'm curious what the unlock was."`

**CTA to your content** - only when the post directly extends a piece
`"Wrote the full breakdown on [your newsletter] - link in first comment."`

**Strong closing statement** - restate the insight memorably
`"The bottleneck was never intelligence. It was delegation with trust."`

Never close with: "What do you think?" / "Let me know your thoughts!" / "Follow for more tips."

## Anti-Patterns

**1. The "see more" trap**
Bad: "I've been reflecting on my journey with tools and wanted to share some thoughts that might be helpful for those of you..."
Good: "I built a content system in 3 weeks. It now runs 80% without me."

**2. Humble-brag opener**
Bad: "So grateful and humbled to share that last month was my best month yet!"
Good: "Last month: $0 in ad spend, 3x growth. Here's the boring system behind it."

**3. Generic list**
Bad: "5 tips to be more productive"
Good: "5 commands I use every day that most people skip"

**4. Instructional over personal**
Bad: "If you want to use AI effectively, you should..."
Good: "I used to prompt AI like I was filling out a form. Output was garbage."

**5. Dropped insight**
Bad: Hook with a number, then never explain it
Good: Hook with a number > story > what the number actually means

**6. Weak closing question**
Bad: "What do you think? Let me know in the comments!"
Good: "What's the first thing you'd automate if you had 3 free hours this week?"

## Platform Notes

- LinkedIn's algorithm rewards dwell time and early engagement (comments > likes)
- Posts with 3-7 line breaks before "see more" perform better than walls
- Best posting times: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am or 12-1pm in your timezone
- Don't post more than once per day - quality over volume

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering:

- [ ] First 2 lines create enough tension to earn "see more"
- [ ] Opens with "I" - personal admission or scene
- [ ] Every paragraph is 1-3 lines with white space
- [ ] Specific numbers, examples, or outcomes - nothing vague
- [ ] No links in body
- [ ] Hashtags at end (3-5)
- [ ] Closing is a genuine question, strong statement, or CTA - never generic
- [ ] Under 300 words unless every word earns its place
- [ ] Sounds like a person, not a content creator
