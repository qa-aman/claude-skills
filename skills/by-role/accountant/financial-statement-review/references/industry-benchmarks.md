# Industry Ratio Benchmarks

Use these benchmarks when comparing ratios in Step 4 of the financial-statement-review workflow.
Note: benchmarks are median ranges. High-growth or capital-intensive companies often deviate legitimately.
Always note when an industry benchmark is being applied and flag if the company's sector is not listed.

---

## SaaS / Software

| Ratio | Healthy Range | Warning Zone | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Gross Margin % | 65-85% | <60% | Below 60% suggests infrastructure cost problem or heavy services mix |
| EBITDA Margin % | 10-25% (mature) | <0% beyond year 3 | Early-stage SaaS may be intentionally negative |
| Revenue Growth YoY | 20-50%+ | <15% (early stage) | Rule of 40: Growth% + EBITDA% should exceed 40 |
| Net Revenue Retention | >110% | <100% | Below 100% = churn exceeds expansion; existential risk |
| CAC Payback Period | <18 months | >24 months | Months of gross profit to recover one customer acquisition cost |
| R&D % of Revenue | 15-25% | >35% | Watch for excessive capitalization inflating margins |
| S&M % of Revenue | 20-40% | >50% | High in land-and-expand models; should decline as company matures |
| AR Days (DSO) | 30-60 days | >90 days | Long DSO in SaaS may indicate collection issues or aggressive rev rec |
| Deferred Revenue | Growing | Declining | Declining deferred revenue often precedes revenue miss |

**SaaS-specific red flags:**
- Net income positive but OCF negative (common in fast-growth, watch trend)
- Revenue growing but gross margin compressing (pricing pressure or cost inflation)
- Deferred revenue declining while revenue is flat or growing (drawing down future revenue)
- Capitalized software as % of total R&D spend increasing YoY (earnings management risk)

## Manufacturing

| Ratio | Healthy Range | Warning Zone | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Gross Margin % | 25-45% | <20% | Varies widely by product type; compare within sub-sector |
| EBITDA Margin % | 8-18% | <5% | Capital-intensive; EBITDA matters more than net income |
| Inventory Turnover | 4-8x | <3x | Low turns indicate demand weakness or obsolete inventory risk |
| Inventory Days | 45-90 days | >120 days | Rising inventory days = demand slowing or over-production |
| AR Days (DSO) | 30-50 days | >75 days | Manufacturing typically has tighter terms than SaaS |
| AP Days | 30-60 days | <20 days | Very short AP days = poor working capital management |
| Cash Conversion Cycle | 30-60 days | >90 days | DSO + Inventory Days - AP Days |
| Debt-to-EBITDA | 1-3x | >4x | Capital-intensive; leverage is expected but must be serviceable |
| Interest Coverage | >3x | <2x | Below 2x is distress territory |
| Asset Turnover | 0.8-1.5x | <0.5x | Low asset turnover = under-utilization of plant and equipment |
| Capex % of Revenue | 3-8% | >12% | Watch for maintenance vs. growth capex split |

**Manufacturing-specific red flags:**
- Inventory growing faster than revenue (demand issue or working capital build)
- Gross margin declining while volumes rise (pricing power loss or input cost spike)
- Capex declining while depreciation rises (underinvestment; future capacity at risk)
- Goodwill > 30% of total assets (acquisition risk; test for impairment indicators)

## Retail

| Ratio | Healthy Range | Warning Zone | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Gross Margin % | 30-55% | <25% | Grocery runs 25%; specialty retail runs 50%+; compare within sub-sector |
| EBITDA Margin % | 5-15% | <3% | Thin margins; small swings have large earnings impact |
| Inventory Turnover | 4-12x | <3x | Fast fashion turns 8-12x; furniture turns 2-4x (normal) |
| Inventory Days | 30-90 days | >120 days | Season-sensitive; compare same-period prior year |
| Same-Store Sales Growth | >0% | Negative for 2+ quarters | Key operating metric; flat or declining signals traffic/conversion problem |
| AR Days (DSO) | 5-20 days | >30 days | Retail is mostly cash/card; high DSO is a red flag |
| Debt-to-EBITDA | 1-3x | >5x | High leverage + thin margins = refinancing risk |
| Lease Obligations | Review ASC 842 disclosures | Operating leverage risk | Post-ASC 842, operating leases on balance sheet; review lease maturity schedule |

**Retail-specific red flags:**
- Inventory turnover slowing (demand softening or markdown risk)
- Gross margin compressing faster than revenue declining (promotional activity destroying margin)
- Same-store sales negative but total revenue growing (new store openings masking base deterioration)
- Operating cash flow significantly below EBITDA (working capital build or lease payments)

## Professional Services (Consulting, Legal, Accounting)

| Ratio | Healthy Range | Warning Zone | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Gross Margin % | 30-50% | <25% | Driven by utilization and bill rate vs. cost rate spread |
| EBITDA Margin % | 10-20% | <8% | Partner comp structures vary; confirm what's above vs. below EBITDA line |
| Revenue Per Employee | $150K-$300K | <$100K | Proxy for billing efficiency and rate realization |
| Utilization Rate | 65-80% | <60% | % of billable hours actually billed; below 60% = overcapacity |
| AR Days (DSO) | 45-75 days | >90 days | Long DSO common; watch for disputed bills or slow-paying clients |
| Revenue Concentration | Top client <15% | >25% single client | High client concentration = key man / key client risk |

---

## Healthcare / Medical

| Ratio | Healthy Range | Warning Zone | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Gross Margin % | 40-65% | <35% | Highly payer-mix dependent |
| EBITDA Margin % | 8-20% | <5% | Regulatory and reimbursement risk affects margins |
| AR Days (DSO) | 40-65 days | >80 days | High DSO often due to payer complexity, not credit risk |
| Days Cash on Hand | >90 days | <30 days | Critical liquidity metric for health systems |
| Debt-to-Capitalization | <50% | >65% | Bond-financed; covenant-heavy |

## When Industry Is Unknown

If the industry is not listed above or is unclear:
- Use the current ratio (>1.5x = healthy), quick ratio (>1.0x), and interest coverage (>3x) as universal stress tests
- Flag the absence of an industry benchmark explicitly in the review memo
- Ask for the company's own prior-period data and use trend analysis in place of external benchmarks
- Reference the company's stated peer group if available in their investor materials
