# Assertions by Financial Statement Area

Use this reference when writing the workpaper header (Step 1) and objective (Step 2).
Match the account being tested to the standard assertions below, then select the
procedures most appropriate for the risk and materiality of the balance.

---

## The Five Balance Sheet Assertions (PCAOB / AICPA)

| Assertion | What It Tests | Key Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Existence** | Assets and liabilities actually exist at the balance sheet date | Is this asset real? Does this liability actually exist? |
| **Completeness** | All assets, liabilities, and transactions that should be recorded are recorded | Is anything missing from the books? |
| **Accuracy / Valuation** | Amounts are recorded at the correct dollar amount and appropriate valuation method | Is this recorded at the right amount? |
| **Rights & Obligations** | The entity has rights to the assets and is obligated for the liabilities | Does the company own this asset? Is this their debt? |
| **Cutoff** | Transactions are recorded in the correct accounting period | Was this recorded in the right period? |
| **Classification / Presentation** | Items are classified and presented appropriately in the financial statements | Is this in the right account and disclosed correctly? |

## Accounts Receivable

**Primary assertions:** Existence, Valuation (net realizable value), Cutoff, Rights

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Existence | Positive confirmation with customers; agree to shipping documents or signed delivery receipts |
| Valuation | Test allowance for doubtful accounts calculation; age the receivables; review subsequent cash collections |
| Cutoff | Test revenue recognition around period end; agree large invoices to shipping dates vs. invoice dates |
| Rights | Review for pledged, factored, or sold receivables; confirm entity has not transferred the asset |
| Completeness | Rarely primary for AR; typically tested through revenue completeness |

**Population note:** Agree AR aging total to GL before selecting sample. Stratify by aging bucket and amount.

## Revenue

**Primary assertions:** Accuracy, Cutoff, Completeness, Existence

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Accuracy | Agree recorded amounts to signed contracts, invoices, and delivery evidence |
| Cutoff | Test transactions within 10-15 business days before and after period end; agree to delivery/acceptance date |
| Completeness | Search for unrecorded revenue: cash receipts after period end that may relate to prior period; confirm deferred revenue roll-forward |
| Existence | Vouch a sample of revenue entries to underlying source documents |

**Revenue recognition note:** For ASC 606, document the five-step model (contract, performance obligations, transaction price, allocation, recognition) for any non-routine or bundled arrangements.

## Accounts Payable

**Primary assertions:** Completeness, Valuation, Cutoff

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Completeness | Vendor statement reconciliations; search for unrecorded payables via AP cutoff test (invoices received after period end for goods/services before period end); confirm large vendor balances |
| Valuation | Agree recorded amounts to vendor invoices; check for unapplied credits or disputed amounts |
| Cutoff | Review receiving reports and vendor invoices around period end; confirm goods received not invoiced (GRNI) accruals |
| Existence | Confirm balances directly with vendors for largest amounts |

**Common exception:** Vendor statement shows higher balance than client books - investigate as potential unrecorded payable (completeness exception). Document individually with investigation and disposition.

## Cash and Bank

**Primary assertions:** Existence, Completeness, Accuracy

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Existence | Obtain bank confirmation directly from financial institution (do not accept client-prepared); agree to bank statement |
| Completeness | Bank reconciliation: confirm all outstanding items are real and clear timely |
| Accuracy | Re-perform the bank reconciliation; agree book balance to GL; agree bank balance to confirmation |

**Bank reconciliation items to scrutinize:**
- Outstanding checks > 90 days (stale; may indicate fictitious payables)
- Deposits in transit > 5 business days (possible window dressing or kiting)
- Reconciling items not described clearly (require explanation and supporting document)

## Inventory

**Primary assertions:** Existence, Completeness, Valuation, Cutoff

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Existence | Observe physical inventory count; test-count a sample and compare to count sheets; compare count sheets to perpetual records |
| Valuation | Test lower of cost or net realizable value (LCNRV); review for slow-moving or obsolete items; confirm cost method (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average) applied consistently |
| Cutoff | Confirm receiving and shipping activity is cut off properly at period end; trace items received before period end to inventory count |
| Completeness | Confirm consignment inventory and goods in transit are included/excluded appropriately |

---

## Fixed Assets (Property, Plant, and Equipment)

**Primary assertions:** Existence, Valuation, Rights, Completeness

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Existence | Physical inspection of significant assets; agree to fixed asset register |
| Valuation | Test depreciation calculations (method, useful life, salvage value consistent with prior periods); test for impairment indicators |
| Rights | Review title documents, lease agreements, and financing statements; confirm assets are not pledged |
| Completeness | Scan expense accounts for items that should be capitalized (betterments vs. repairs); confirm additions agree to purchase documents |

**Capitalization policy:** Confirm the client has a documented capitalization threshold and it has been applied consistently.

---

## Long-Term Debt

**Primary assertions:** Existence, Completeness, Accuracy, Rights & Obligations

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Existence / Completeness | Confirm balances directly with lenders; obtain debt agreements and trace to balance sheet |
| Accuracy | Recalculate interest expense and accrued interest; agree amortization schedule to balance |
| Rights & Obligations | Confirm the entity is the obligor; review for debt covenants and confirm compliance |

**Disclosure:** Verify current vs. non-current classification (any debt due within 12 months must be current); confirm covenant violations disclosed if applicable.

---

## Equity

**Primary assertions:** Completeness, Accuracy, Rights & Obligations

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Accuracy | Agree opening balance to prior year; trace transactions (stock issuances, dividends, repurchases) to board minutes and cash records |
| Completeness | Confirm all equity transactions are recorded; review board minutes for undisclosed dividends or capital transactions |
| Rights & Obligations | Review articles of incorporation; confirm authorized, issued, and outstanding shares agree to transfer agent records |

---

## Accrued Liabilities

**Primary assertions:** Completeness, Valuation, Cutoff

| Assertion | Standard Procedures |
|---|---|
| Completeness | Agree accrual schedule to supporting documentation; search for unrecorded accruals via expense analysis and contracts review |
| Valuation | Test estimates: payroll accrual (agree to payroll calendar), warranty accrual (review claims history and methodology), litigation accrual (obtain legal confirmation letter) |
| Cutoff | Confirm accruals are based on services/goods received before period end |

**Legal letter:** For litigation and contingency accruals, always send a legal confirmation letter to the client's outside counsel. Do not rely solely on management's representation.
